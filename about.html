What are modal verbs?

Modal Verbs Overview

Modal verbs are auxiliary verbs that express ability, possibility, permission, necessity, or advice.

Can

Use: Ability, permission, possibility.

Example: I can swim very fast.

Could

Use: Past ability, polite permission or possibility.

Example: Could you help me with this task?

May

Use: Formal permission or possibility.

Example: You may leave early today.

Might

Use: Small possibility.

Example: It might rain tomorrow.

Must

Use: Obligation or strong recommendation.

Example: You must wear a seatbelt.

Shall

Use: Offers or suggestions (formal).

Example: Shall we dance?

Should

Use: Advice or recommendation.

Example: You should drink more water.

Will

Use: Future intentions or promises.

Example: I will call you later.

Would

Use: Polite requests or hypothetical situations.

Example: Would you like some coffee?
